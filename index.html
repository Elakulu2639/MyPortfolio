About

I build for clarity,
change, and real use.

I am a software engineering graduate focused on building practical full-stack products that solve real operational problems.

I enjoy building software that has a clear purpose: systems that help people organize work, understand data, manage business processes, or replace slow manual workflows. My projects have given me practice across complete application flows, from shaping the user experience to designing data models, APIs, and backend behavior.

When I approach a problem, I try to understand the workflow before deciding on the implementation. I care about what the user is trying to accomplish, what the business needs to measure or control, and where the system is likely to change later. That helps me make decisions that are simpler, easier to test, and less fragile.

Clean architecture matters to me because software rarely stays in its first version. I want the systems I build to be understandable after the first release, safe to extend, and useful to the people who depend on them. I am still early in my professional career, but I am intentional about learning from every project, improving how I structure code, and building with long-term reliability in mind.
